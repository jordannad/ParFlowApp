Parflow.CLM is an open-source, 3D variably saturated overland flow-subsurface model developed by Reed Maxwell at the Colorado School of Mines. It is often computationally expensive to solve the 3D Richards Equation for complex topography so the model allows for parallelization across CPU cores.

This exercise for CEE166A/266A students simulates the McClellan Creek watershed delineated in Exercise 1. The grid cells are 100m by 100m to reduce the computational and memory requirements of the model.
